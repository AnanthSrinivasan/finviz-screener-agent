## Weekly Momentum Brief — April 20–24, 2026

Frame them accordingly.

**Why Each Name Ranks Here**

**ARM (score 280, #1)** earns the top slot because this isn't a trade — it's a narrative regime change. On March 24, at the "Arm Everywhere" conference, ARM announced its AGI CPU, a self-designed chip targeting AI data centers directly rather than just licensing IP. That single event broke a decade-long business model and the market is repricing the entire earnings power profile: analysts are penciling in $15B of AGI CPU revenue by 2031 and $25B total. The stock ran from $137 to $234 in roughly three weeks. This week's additional 14% move came when Intel's results confirmed record CPU demand, providing third-party validation of exactly the thesis ARM is pitching. The IPO lifecycle bonus applies because ARM is still relatively young as a public company at this scale; the 52-week high, Power Move, and Week 20%+ Gain screeners all fired simultaneously. The character change flags are fully earned — 200-day gain exceeding 50%, RVol above 2.5x, and a business model inflection that institutional money has to underwrite. The risk: it's reporting fiscal Q4 soon, and a stock that's moved this far, this fast, on forward revenue guidance that doesn't materialize until 2028 is carrying enormous expectations. Extended, but the setup is real.

**MXL (score 275, #2)** is the cleanest fundamental story in the group. This is a textbook episodic pivot: Q1 2026 earnings dropped April 23–25 with 136% infrastructure revenue growth, a $0.04 EPS beat, and Q2 guidance of $160–170M against a $137M Street estimate. The stock responded with its largest single-day percentage gain in company history, roughly 80%. That kind of gap on that kind of volume — every momentum screener firing at once — is exactly what the system is designed to catch. Needham upgraded to Buy at $60, Roth MKM upgraded to Buy at $60, Stifel raised to $49, and even the Hold-rated Susquehanna moved its target to $45 from $30. The forward narrative is anchored in optical data-center connectivity, which has "significant multi-quarter growth visibility" per institutional commentary. Q99 quality rank, 5/5 persistence, and the highest base score in the group. This is the name that would be first into the cart when the market state clears.

**NVTS (score 265, #3)** is a different animal — more speculative, higher risk, but the catalyst stack is real. Navitas appointed a former Broadcom exec to its board on April 13, announced a May 5 earnings date, and showcased an 800V GaN power delivery solution at NVIDIA GTC and APEC targeting AI server infrastructure. The direct NVIDIA data-center narrative plus a May 5 binary event drove a 16% move this week on top of significant short squeeze dynamics — over 25% of the float was short, and volume hit ~87.7M shares, which is forced repositioning layered on top of speculative conviction. The "Navitas 2.0" pivot toward high-power GaN/SiC for AI and grid targets a $3.5B SAM by 2030, and high-power markets hit majority revenue contribution for the first time in Q4. **The critical caveat**: Q87 rank, negative EPS (-24.8%), and a stock that's pricing a large future AI opportunity into a pre-revenue inflection. May 5 earnings is a binary event — if the quarter doesn't narrow the narrative-to-revenue gap, this retraces hard. Watch only until post-earnings clarity.

**POET (score 265, #3 tied)** and **CAR (score 225, #5)** round out the list. POET is a Q99-ranked semiconductor name with 34.7% EPS growth, hitting 52-week highs on Power Move and 10% Change screeners — the optical interconnect theme that's also lifting MXL is the likely sector tailwind here. CAR (Avis Budget) is the outlier — an industrial/rental name with 51.2% EPS growth, but only 3/5 persistence days and a 19.0% ATR, which is brutal to size correctly. CAR belongs to a different trade: episodic pivot on earnings or sector rotation into industrials, not a semiconductor momentum continuation. The high ATR makes position sizing punishing, and the sector context (industrials vs. the semis rip) is less clean. **CAR is watch only — not actionable** until persistence improves and ATR compresses.

The dominant theme is unmistakable: SMH +9.1% on the week, XLK +3.8%, QQQ +2.3% — semiconductors are the leadership group and the top four names are all sitting in that sector. The AGI infrastructure buildout is the organizing narrative, and it's hitting from multiple angles simultaneously: ARM's CPU pivot, MXL's optical connectivity blowout, NVTS's GaN power for AI servers, and POET in optical interconnects. These aren't random names — they're all exposed to the same capex cycle from hyperscalers building out AI data centers. Oil's 14.1% week (USO) adds an energy infrastructure subtext. The headwinds: gold and silver both down 3–7% this week, biotech down 3.9%, and financials off 1.9% — rotation out of defensive and inflation-hedge plays and into growth. TLT flat, dollar up slightly — not a rate-shock environment. Bitcoin flatlined for the week despite MSTR's strong month. The macro picture is broadly supportive for momentum growth names, but the 5-day ratio at 0.87 says the tape needs to prove it can hold the semiconductor gains before new entries make sense.
