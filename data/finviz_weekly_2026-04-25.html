## Weekly Momentum Brief — April 20–24, 2026

**Market State: RED | Blackout — No Monday Entries**

The macro tape is split but the internals are telling. QQQ ripped 2.32% on the week and SMH exploded 9.11% — semiconductors are clearly the epicenter of institutional money flow right now. But the broader market is not confirming: the 5-day ratio sits at 0.87, well below the 1.5 threshold needed to flip to green, even as the 10-day ratio at 1.81 shows the underlying thrust from two weeks ago is still alive. Until the 5-day ratio clears 1.5 and we see another thrust confirmation, this is a blackout week — all five names below are **watch only, not actionable Monday**.

---

**The Top 5 — Why They Rank Here**

**ARM (signal score 280)** tops the board for a reason that goes beyond screeners: this is a full business model transformation being repriced in real time. ARM unveiled its first self-designed AGI CPU at its March "Arm Everywhere" conference, pivoting from pure IP royalty licensing to selling finished silicon directly into AI data centers — a category shift the market is only beginning to underwrite. Revenue from that chip doesn't start until 2028 and doesn't scale toward $15B until 2031, but Wall Street isn't waiting: Citi, Guggenheim, Evercore, Mizuho, Barclays, Needham, and Susquehanna all raised targets or upgraded, with top targets hitting $240. ARM then added a 14% single-session gain on April 24 when Intel's earnings confirmed record CPU demand — a sector read-across that poured fuel on an already-running fire. CEO Rene Haas simultaneously taking the helm of SoftBank Group International puts ARM's leadership at the center of SoftBank's direct Nvidia-competitive ambitions. The stock went from $136.96 on March 30 to $234.81 on April 24 — roughly 71% in 18 trading days. The IPO lifecycle bonus and 52-week high flag are both valid, but the real driver is a narrative inflection backed by a seven-firm analyst upgrade cycle. Massively extended right now; the ATR is 5% on a stock that just moved 71% — any entry here without a constructive base forming is chasing into air.

**MXL (signal score 275)** is the cleanest fundamental catalyst in the group. MaxLinear reported Q1 results and the headline that moved the stock was infrastructure revenue up 136% year-over-year — not sequentially, year-over-year. EPS of $0.22 beat the $0.18 consensus, but more importantly Q2 guidance came in at $160–170M versus the $137M Street estimate, and the company raised its full-year optical data center revenue outlook by $40M. CEO Kishore Seendripu called it a "clear inflection point" in the optical data center connectivity business — the AI buildout is consuming optical interconnect at a pace that's pulling MXL forward faster than its own prior estimates. Needham upgraded to Buy at $60, Roth MKM upgraded to Buy at $60, and Stifel lifted its target to $49. Q99 rank with 46% EPS growth and 5/5 days of persistence — this is the highest-quality fundamental setup in the cohort. The stock's biggest single-day percentage gain in company history triggered the Power Move and Week 20%+ Gain screeners simultaneously. Like ARM, it is extended post-earnings gap — needs time to build or a tight flag before it's enterable.

**NVTS (signal score 265)** is a different animal — a high-conviction momentum trade with meaningful short-squeeze mechanics layered underneath. Navitas appointed a former Broadcom executive to its board on April 13, then announced Q1 earnings on May 5, giving the market both a governance credibility signal and a near-term event to position around. The real fuel is GaNFast technology for AI server power delivery — specifically an 800V–6V DC-DC board hitting 96.5% peak efficiency at 2,100 W/in³, showcased at NVIDIA GTC 2026. With more than a quarter of the float short and volume surging to nearly 87.7M shares, the 16% single-session move has forced-covering fingerprints all over it. The pivot to high-power AI data center markets is real ("Navitas 2.0"), and the GlobalFoundries foundry agreement adds a reshoring/tariff tailwind. The critical caveat: EPS is -24.8%, Q87 is the lowest rank in the group, and valuation has outrun monetization. A decent May 5 earnings report that doesn't narrow the narrative-to-revenue gap meaningfully could be sold hard. Flag this as extended with event risk — watch the May 5 print before considering any entry.

**POET (signal score 265)** ties NVTS on score but carries Q99 — the highest Quant rank in the cohort alongside MXL. POET Technologies is an optical semiconductor company whose integrated photonics platform sits directly in the path of AI data center interconnect demand. The 10% Change, 52-Week High, Power Move, and Week 20%+ Gain screener stack all fired simultaneously, consistent with an episodic pivot driven by sector re-rating rather than a single earnings print. EPS growth of 34.7% with Q99 rank puts this in a different fundamental category than NVTS. ATR at 8.9% signals this is a smaller, more volatile name — position sizing needs to reflect that. Worth watching closely for a base-building setup once market conditions normalize.

**CAR (signal score 225)** is the outlier — the only non-semiconductor name, an Industrials/Rental & Leasing business that still scored 225 on the signal board. The 51.2% EPS growth and ATR of 19% tell a story of a deeply dislocated stock snapping back hard. CAR only showed up on 3/5 days of persistence (vs. 5/5 for the semis), which is the right flag — the move is real but less sustained. The sector tailwind here is likely USO's 14.1% weekly gain creating a macro narrative around travel/transportation demand, combined with genuine earnings improvement. The 19% ATR makes this a wide-stop, high-volatility trade — not for tight risk management setups. Watch only until persistence improves and the broader market confirms.

**Sector Themes and Macro Backdrop**

Semiconductors are doing the heavy lifting and the concentration is notable: four of the top five names are in semis, SMH is up 9.11% on the week and 26.92% on the month, and the AI infrastructure buildout is the single most powerful fundamental theme in the market right now. ARM's AGI CPU pivot, MXL's optical interconnect inflection, NVTS's GaNFast AI server power, and POET's photonics platform are all variations on the same trade — AI data centers are consuming every layer of the semiconductor stack simultaneously. The Intel earnings catalyst that added 14% to ARM on April 24 shows how interconnected these names are; a rising tide from any hyperscaler or CPU print can lift the entire cohort. The risk to this theme is valuation running ahead of revenue realization — ARM's $15B chip revenue target is a 2031 story, MXL's inflection is real but the stock already moved 80% in a week. On the macro side, gold and silver selling off while tech rips is consistent with a risk-on rotation out of defensives, but Financials and Biotech lagging suggests this is not a broad bull move — it's a concentrated semiconductor/AI bet. TLT flat and dollar slightly up means no macro tailwind from rates or currency. The tape is telling you to be in the right sector, not just long the market.
