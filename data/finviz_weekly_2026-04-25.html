## Weekly Momentum Brief — April 20–24, 2026

**Market Context First**

The market state is RED with a 5-day ratio at 0.87, which means the short-term breadth has collapsed even as the 10-day ratio sits at a healthy 1.81 — a sign that last week's strength was a burst inside a still-fragile structure, not a clean re-acceleration. The macro picture is genuinely split: SMH ripped 9.11% on the week and XLK added 3.80%, confirming that semiconductors are the leadership sector right now, but financials (-1.93%), biotech (-3.89%), and precious metals (gold -2.84%, silver -6.57%) are in rotation-out mode. Fear & Greed sits at 66, Greed, but came down slightly from 68 last week — not a warning sign on its own, but combined with the RED market state and sub-1.0 five-day ratio, the rules are clear: no new entries Monday. All five names below are names to watch when breadth confirms, not names to chase at the open.

**The Top 5 — Real Catalysts, Not Just Screener Hits**

ARM ranks first at a signal score of 280 because it is undergoing a genuine business model transformation, not just a hot week. The "Arm Everywhere" event in March revealed the AGI CPU — ARM's first fully self-designed chip sold directly to data center customers rather than licensed IP — with Meta as lead partner, OpenAI and Cloudflare already committed, and $1B in contracted 2028 revenue. Management guided to $25B in total revenue by 2031 versus roughly $4B in 2025, a 5-6x ambition the market is repricing in real time. This week the Intel earnings print confirming record CPU demand added sector confirmation, and ARM popped 14% on April 24 alone. The IPO lifecycle bonus reflects the stock still being in its early institutional accumulation phase post-2023 listing. The 52-week high, Power Move, and Week 20%+ Gain screeners are all downstream of that single thesis: the market is upgrading ARM from a royalty business to a full-stack AI infrastructure company.

MXL ranks second at 275 and is the purest earnings-driven episodic pivot in the list. The stock surged roughly 80% on Friday after Q1 results that beat estimates and guidance that shocked the street — Q2 revenue guided to $160-170M versus the $137M consensus, and management raised full-year optical data center revenue by $40M. The number that broke the model for bears was 136% infrastructure revenue year-over-year growth. Four analyst upgrades or target raises followed immediately, with Needham and Roth MKM both initiating Buy at $60. This is a genuine character change: consensus is now modeling $0.91 EPS for 2026 versus a loss of $1.58 in the prior year, which means funds that were underweight or short have to reposition. The Q99 rank and 5/5 persistence confirm institutional buying has been systematic, not one-day noise. NVTS and POET both rank at 265 and share the semiconductor AI theme but with important differences. NVTS is a short-squeeze plus AI positioning story, not an earnings beat — the real catalyst is the "Navitas 2.0" pivot to high-power GaN and SiC for AI data centers and grid applications, a GlobalFoundries domestic manufacturing deal, and a high-short-float setup where 87.7 million shares traded in a single session as shorts were forced to reposition. The Q1 2026 earnings report on May 5 is the next binary event and the key risk. POET is a Q99 name with 34.7% EPS growth and the same semiconductor AI tailwind, and its 8.9% ATR reflects genuine speculative positioning in optical interconnect technology directly relevant to data center scaling. CAR rounds out the list at 225, the lone non-semiconductor name, and its episodic pivot is a genuine surprise — 51.2% EPS growth in rental and leasing, a character change confirmed by RVol over 2.5x and a 200-day gain over 50%. The high ATR of 19% flags this as a volatile, lower-conviction setup compared to the semiconductor names, and the 3/5 day persistence versus 5/5 for the others is worth noting.

**Sector Themes and Macro Backdrop**

The semiconductor sector is the undisputed leader right now and the thesis is internally consistent: ARM's custom silicon ambitions, MXL's optical interconnect inflection, NVTS's GaN power density play, and POET's photonics work are all direct infrastructure layers of the same AI data center buildout. SMH's 9.11% weekly gain and 26.92% monthly gain confirm this is not just individual stock noise — it is a sector-wide repricing of AI capex expectations. USO's 14.10% weekly surge is notable and bears watching for cost pressures on industrial names, but it does not directly threaten the semiconductor thesis. The dollar's slight recovery (+0.44%) and TLT's flat week mean rates are not the immediate headwind. The real risk to all five names is that the RED market state and 5-day ratio at 0.87 mean the broad tape is not confirming the semiconductor leadership — if SPY rolls over further, even Q99 names will see air pockets.

**Monday Plan — Watch, Don't Chase**

Market state is RED and the 5-day ratio at 0.87 is well below the 1.5 threshold needed to justify new entries. All five names are Stage 2 Uptrend with Q-ranks from 87 to 107 and qualify structurally, but the rules require breadth confirmation before pulling the trigger. The re-entry signal to watch is the 5-day ratio crossing back above 1.5 with at least one new thrust day — ideally SPY reclaiming its short-term moving average on above-average volume. When that signal fires, the order of priority is MXL first (cleanest fundamental catalyst, institutional forced repositioning still in early innings, 80% single-day move with volume confirms the pivot), ARM second (business model transformation with named customers and multi-year revenue roadmap, 14% day on Intel confirmation), POET third (Q99 with strong EPS, lower ATR than NVTS), NVTS fourth (binary event May 5 earnings creates defined catalyst but also binary risk — size accordingly and know the date), and CAR fifth (real earnings power but 19% ATR and lone-sector status make it lower priority when four semiconductor names are all firing in the same theme). On each name, the entry trigger is a base or tight flag forming above the episodic gap — do not chase extended opens. Confirmation day, not gap-and-go day, is the entry.
