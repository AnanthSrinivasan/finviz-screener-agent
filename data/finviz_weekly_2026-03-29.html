## Weekly Momentum Brief — March 24–27, 2026

**Market State: RED / BLACKOUT**

Before getting into the names, the macro backdrop demands the lead. SPY is down 8% on the month, QQQ down nearly 8%, and the Fear & Greed index sits at 10.2 — Extreme Fear. SPY is trading below its 200-day moving average with no thrust event in the last 30 days. The 5-day and 10-day advance/decline ratios are both flat at 1.0, offering zero breadth confirmation. What this week's top names represent is a ranked watchlist for when conditions recover. That context shapes which names here have the best re-entry case.

**The Top 5 — Why They're Here**

GEV leads the board at a signal score of 165 and earns it on fundamentals, not just price action. The real-world driver is a convergence of three separate catalysts: a massive earnings beat ($13.39 EPS vs. $2.99 consensus), a blowout 2026 guide ($41–42B revenue, $4.5–5.0B free cash flow), and a rare double-upgrade from Rothschild & Co Redburn — Sell to Buy, price target from $560 to $1,100 — all tied to AI data center electricity demand pulling forward gas turbine orders at an accelerating pace. The $150B backlog and 65% order growth year-over-year are not noise; they represent multi-year revenue visibility. The 52W High screener hit is organic — this stock is breaking out because the earnings power inflected, not because of a thematic narrative. The IPO lifecycle signal is relevant given GEV's relatively recent spin-off history. ATR of 4.7% means it moves, and with a Q52 rating it sits at the very top of the transitional tier. That said, market state is RED — this is the first name back on the list when breadth recovers.

ATR of 7.5% means position sizing must be disciplined. Q51 — high transitional, but not yet Stage 2 confirmed.
