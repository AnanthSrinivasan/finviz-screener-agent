## 1. TOP CONVICTION SETUPS

SNDK is the single highest conviction name on the board this week, appearing all 7 days across three distinct screeners including 52 Week High, Growth, and IPO. That trifecta tells a complete story: this is a recently public or re-listed name with strong fundamental momentum that is simultaneously making new highs. The market is under meaningful pressure right now with SPY down over 5% on the month, so a name that is still printing 52-week highs in this environment is not just holding — it is actively outperforming. SNDK sits in Computer Hardware within Technology, a sector that is relatively resilient with XLK only down 3.5% on the month and SMH holding better than the broad market. This is the top priority name going into next week.

CIEN is equally compelling. Seven days, three screeners, and it combines the 10% Change screener with 52 Week High and Growth. The 10% Change screener hitting alongside new highs means this name had a significant single-week price event and held or extended it through the rest of the week. Communication Equipment has been a quiet beneficiary of ongoing infrastructure and networking buildout narratives, and CIEN is one of the more established names in that space. The persistence here is exceptional — seven days of consecutive screener presence in a week where most momentum names are getting smoked by the macro environment.

VG is the most interesting outlier in the top three. It sits in Oil & Gas Midstream and is showing up across 10% Change, Growth, and IPO screeners all week. Energy is the one sector with genuine macro tailwind right now — XLE is up 2.79% this week and 7.48% on the month, and USO crude oil is up nearly 50% over the past month. That is an extraordinary commodity move. A midstream name clearing 10% in a single week while energy is running this hard and simultaneously hitting Growth and IPO screeners suggests this could be a smaller or newer vehicle for capturing energy infrastructure exposure. The macro wind is directly at its back, which separates it from almost everything else on this list.

BW rounds out the top tier. Six out of seven days, four screeners including a Week 20%+ Gain flag, 10% Change, 52 Week High, and Growth. The 20%+ weekly gain screener is significant — that level of price appreciation in a single week in a broadly down market is a major signal. BW is in Specialty Industrial Machinery, which connects to capital expenditure and infrastructure themes that have been persistent in this market cycle. The fact that it showed four screener hits simultaneously and nearly perfect weekly persistence despite being in a non-sexy industrial category makes this a name that deserves serious attention.

LASR and MU both deserve mention as secondary tier setups. LASR in Semiconductors with 52 Week High and Growth on seven consecutive days is notable because SMH as a sector ETF is only down 0.67% this week despite the broader tape being ugly — semiconductors are showing relative strength and LASR making new highs within that sector is exactly the kind of leadership you want to follow. MU is the large-cap version of the same thesis, a bellwether semiconductor name holding 52-week highs across all seven days, which tells you institutional accumulation is present and not retreating.
