## Weekly Brief: March 15–21, 2026

The macro backdrop is ugly but the focus list is doing its own thing entirely. Broad market is in distribution — SPY down 2% on the week, off 5% on the month, Fear & Greed sitting at 14.6 which is deep Extreme Fear territory. Commodities got hit hard with gold and silver both cratering double digits. The only pockets with genuine bid are energy (XLE +2.8%, USO +1.3%) and financials barely holding flat. Against that backdrop, finding names running up 10-20% in a week and hitting 52-week highs is rare and meaningful. When the market is selling everything and certain names are still making new highs, that's exactly the kind of relative strength that precedes outsized moves once conditions stabilize.

SNDK and CIEN are the two highest-conviction names on the board. SNDK hitting all three screeners — 52-Week High, Growth, and IPO — in the same session means this is fresh institutional sponsorship on a recently listed name breaking out to new highs with fundamental backing. IPO names that build a base and then surge to new highs in a weak tape are prime candidates for extended moves because the float is still thin and early holders are sitting on gains rather than bailing. Watch for continuation above the week's high on volume. CIEN is the episodic pivot of the week. Gap or surge into a new 52-week high, tagged by 10% Change, 52 Week High, and Growth simultaneously — that's the classic EP signature where a catalyst (likely earnings revision, guidance raise, or sector contract news) rerates the stock to a new level. The move isn't noise; it's price discovery. The playbook is simple: if CIEN holds the gap level Monday, that's the long trigger. If it fades back into the gap, wait for reclaim.

VG and BW are both worth sizing. VG catches the eye because it's an Energy/Midstream name screaming 10% Change, Growth, and IPO together — midstream is already getting macro tailwinds from the energy complex (USO nearly up 50% on the month), and a freshly listed name in that space with growth characteristics hitting these screens simultaneously suggests institutional accumulation. The IPO angle again means limited overhead supply. BW is an episodic pivot in Industrials — gap or surge into a 52-week high plus a weekly 20%+ gain is not a gentle drift higher, that's a violent repricing. Four screeners firing same day including Week 20%+ Gain tells you this week's move was explosive. The risk with BW is it's already extended after a 20% week, so the entry discipline matters — pull back to the breakout level or first tight day after the gap, don't chase the extended candle.

AXTI is the highest-risk, highest-reward name on the list. Semiconductor equipment, 20%+ week, new 52-week high, in a tape where SMH only dropped 0.7% — semi equipment holding up while broader tech fades is notable. Five out of seven days in the screeners with two firing same day on an EP setup tells you the move has persistence, not just a one-day spike. Semi equipment names can run hard when cycle inflection narratives take hold. The trigger for adding Monday is whether AXTI consolidates near the high without giving up more than a half-ATR on lower volume. Any tight inside day or low-volume pullback is the setup.

Sector themes are straightforward this week. Energy is the only sector with genuine macro wind at its back — USO nearly doubling over a month is a commodity supercycle signal and midstream benefits from throughput volume. Tech is hanging in better than expected given the fear reading; the fact that SMH held and XLK only dropped 1% while everything else got hit harder means institutions are not fully abandoning the growth trade. The gold/silver collapse is a head-scratcher in a fear environment — likely forced liquidation or a strong dollar correction that overcame safe-haven demand. Crypto is range-bound and not a distraction this week.

Monday morning the priority list is CIEN first, SNDK second. On CIEN, if it opens at or above last week's close and volume comes in above average in the first 30 minutes, that's a long entry with a stop under the gap. Target is a measured move equal to the EP gap. On SNDK, look for the first consolidation candle after the open — if the morning range is tight and volume is light, that's an add or initiation point. On BW, do not chase — only enter if it pulls back to the breakout zone or gives a tight inside day setup. On VG, similar patience, but given the energy macro it could gap up Monday on any oil news, so have a buy-stop above the week's high ready. AXTI is a watch on Monday, not an immediate trigger — let it show its hand for the first hour before committing.
