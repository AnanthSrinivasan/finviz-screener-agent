## Weekly Brief: March 15–21, 2026

**Focus Names**

SNDK leads the list with perfect 7/7 day presence and the rare triple-screen combination of 52 Week High, Growth, and IPO. That IPO tag is the key — this is a freshly public name printing new highs immediately out of the gate, which is the single most reliable momentum signature in the playbook. The market is telling you institutional demand is overwhelming any early seller overhang. Watch for a clean consolidation near the highs on light volume early next week — that's the entry. Any wide-range day closing near the top of the bar on volume is the trigger to get aggressive. CIEN is the standout episodic pivot of the week. A gap plus new 52-week high plus 10% single-day change plus Growth screen all firing simultaneously is a textbook EP signature — something fundamental shifted in how the market perceives this name, whether that's earnings, a guidance raise, or a contract win. The gap matters because it's trapped shorts and forced capitulation buying above a level that had previously acted as resistance. Watch the gap fill level as your risk line — if it holds on any early-week pullback, CIEN is a buy. If it fills the gap completely, the thesis is broken and you step aside.

VG catches the eye as an energy midstream name showing up on 10% Change, Growth, and IPO screens simultaneously with perfect attendance. Midstream is not where you expect explosive growth momentum, which makes this stand out harder — something structural is happening, likely tied to the oil surge (USO up nearly 50% month-over-month). A freshly public midstream company printing growth-screen numbers in this oil tape is a compelling combination. Watch for continuation above whatever the breakout level was on the IPO screen trigger — midstream names can trend smoothly once institutions get positioned. BW is an episodic pivot in industrials showing up on four screeners including the Week 20%+ Gain tag, which confirms this isn't a one-day wonder — the move has been sustained across the week. Specialty industrial machinery in a market where most things are red is a significant relative strength statement. The 52 Week High plus the weekly gain confirms accumulation, not distribution. AXTI rounds out the list as the weakest conviction of the five but still an EP setup — semiconductors hitting new highs and up 20%+ in a week while SMH is only down fractionally tells you this is stock-specific, not sector-driven. That's the kind of idiosyncratic strength worth owning.

That context makes VG and any energy-adjacent name more interesting. The precious metals flush is notable — GLD down 10% on the week, GDX down 14%, SLV down 15% — that's a crowded trade unwinding fast, not a rotation. Crypto is effectively flat on the week and hanging in, which is a mild positive given the risk-off pressure everywhere else. The bottom line on macro: you are swimming against a strong current. Position sizes should reflect that. The focus names on this list are showing genuine relative strength in a down tape, which is exactly when you want to be selective rather than broad.

Stay disciplined on size — the market is in Extreme Fear and even the best setups fail more often in this environment. Let the setups come to you.
