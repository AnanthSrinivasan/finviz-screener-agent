CIEN and VG are the two strongest EP candidates because they combine maximum persistence with EP qualification. VG is particularly interesting because it sits in Oil and Gas Midstream, and energy is the single strongest sector this week with XLE up 2.79% and USO up 1.28% on the week with a stunning 49.56% monthly gain. VG firing both 10% Change and Growth screeners in an energy sector that is actually working with macro tailwind behind it makes this one of the cleaner EP setups on the board. The gap or surge that triggered the EP flag likely had a fundamental catalyst — possibly a pipeline deal, contract win, or production update — and the sector is confirming the move.

BW is the next EP candidate worth building a case around. Six of seven days visible, firing across four screeners including the Week 20%+ Gain flag. That 20% weekly gain screener is the most important signal here — it means the stock moved violently at some point this week, and it was still showing up on persistence screeners every subsequent day. In Specialty Industrial Machinery, that kind of move usually traces to an earnings beat, a large contract announcement, or a spinoff-related rerating. The sustained follow-through after the initial surge is exactly what Stockbee EP methodology looks for: the gap holds, buyers step in on every dip, and the stock does not give back the move.

AXTI is a Semiconductor Equipment name that fired 10% Change, 52 Week High, and Week 20%+ Gain across five of seven days. This is a lower-cap, higher-volatility name and the EP setup here is more aggressive. Semi equipment stocks have been early-cycle indicators historically, and AXTI hitting new highs with a 20%+ weekly surge in a difficult tape suggests something specific happened — likely an order announcement or analyst upgrade tied to capacity expansion. The 5/7 persistence tells you the move is not fully exhausted.

LITE is worth watching even at 4/7 persistence because it combines 10% Change, 52 Week High, and Growth in the Technology sector. Four days is lower conviction, but for an EP setup you only need confirmation that the initial move is holding. LONA at 1/7 is purely speculative and only relevant if you see it on the tape Monday with volume.

## 3. SECTOR THEMES

Energy is the dominant theme and the only sector with clear macro support right now. XLE up 2.79% on the week, USO up nearly 50% over the month, and multiple energy names appearing in persistence screeners including VG, AR, and EQT. The macro story is simple: oil is running hard, energy stocks are lagging the commodity move on a monthly basis which suggests there is still catch-up potential, and the sector is attracting momentum flows as investors rotate out of technology and into real assets.

Technology is showing internal divergence rather than broad strength. The index is down, but specific names hitting 52-week highs — SNDK, CIEN, ADEA, MU, LASR — are holding up in a way that suggests selective institutional buying rather than sector-wide momentum. Semiconductors specifically are the most resilient tech subsector this week. The names on the persistence leaderboard in tech are not random — they cluster around AI infrastructure, optical networking, and storage, which are the sub-themes receiving actual capital allocation.

Industrials is worth watching given BW and KRMN both appearing with strength. KRMN is Aerospace and Defense, which benefits from ongoing defense spending themes globally. BW's week 20%+ gain flags a potential rerating event in Specialty Machinery.
