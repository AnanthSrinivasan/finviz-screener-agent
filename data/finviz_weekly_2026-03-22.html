## Weekly Momentum Review — March 16–21, 2026

**Top Names Breakdown**

BW leads the board at 210 with the cleanest episodic pivot of the week. The real catalyst was the March 4 announcement of a $2.4 billion notice-to-proceed with Base Electron and Applied Digital for 1.2 GW of natural gas power for AI factory campuses — that single event drove a 46% single-day gap, pushed backlog up 470% to $2.8 billion, and triggered the 10% Change, 52-Week High, Growth, and Week 20%+ Gain screeners simultaneously. The EBITDA beat (106% growth to $43.7M) and Northland's aggressive estimate revisions gave fundamental legs to the move, and insider buys from both CEO and CFO in the days following confirm conviction from the inside. This is not a rumor play — it's a $2.4 billion signed contract with identifiable counterparties. CIEN scores 200 on a similar EP structure. Ciena's Q1 FY2026 print was the trigger: $1.43B revenue up 33% YoY, EPS of $1.35 against a $1.14 estimate — a 111% YoY earnings growth number. Shares initially crashed 12% on the print, which is the shakeout, and then spent the week recovering hard as B of A flipped from Neutral to Buy, JP Morgan held Overweight with a $380 target, and the OFC 2026 conference gave management a stage to demo 1.6T AI networking hardware in real time. Cloud providers drove 42% of revenue, up 76% YoY — this is an AI infrastructure name, not a legacy telecom equipment story.

SNDK ranks third at 195, driven by the IPO lifecycle signal following its clean separation from Western Digital. The spinoff was finalized February 24, 2025, and the final liquidation of Western Digital's remaining equity stake on February 18, 2026 removed the supply overhang entirely — that's the technical event that restarted institutional accumulation. The fundamental case backs it up: Q2 FY2026 revenue of $3.03 billion grew 61% YoY, EPS came in at $6.20 against a $3.49 estimate — a 75% beat — with datacenter revenues up 76% YoY. Citi raised to $875 post-Micron earnings as a read-through, and KGI initiated at $992 Outperform. S&P 500 inclusion on top of overhang clearance is a textbook post-spinoff re-rating setup. VG scores 185, also IPO lifecycle tagged, showing up in 10% Change, Growth, and IPO screeners all week in the energy midstream space — notable given oil's strength (USO up 49% over the past month), which is providing direct sector support for a new midstream name ramping through its early public lifecycle. KRMN rounds out the list at 155 as the weakest signal of the five — Growth and IPO screeners only, no episodic pivot, no 52-week high bonus. It's an Aerospace & Defense name benefiting from the defense spending tailwind but without the clean catalyst structure of the top four. Lower conviction, smaller position sizing warranted.

**Sector Themes and Macro Backdrop**

The macro environment is openly hostile to broad long exposure. SPY is down 2% on the week and 5.25% on the month, IWM is off 8.5% in a month, and Fear & Greed sits at 14.6 — deep Extreme Fear territory that has moved from 44 just a month ago. Broad risk-off is the dominant condition. What's working against that backdrop tells you everything: energy (XLE +2.8% week, +7.5% month, USO +49% month) is the clearest sector winner, which directly supports VG in midstream. Technology is holding up better than small caps — XLK down only 1.1% on the week, SMH down 0.67% — which gives cover to CIEN and SNDK as AI infrastructure and memory names with real earnings behind them. The commodity flush (SLV -15%, GLD -10%, GDX -14% on the week) is dramatic and confirms rotation away from inflation hedges, but that's not where these names live. BW is interesting because it bridges industrials and energy infrastructure — a $2.4 billion AI power contract is effectively an energy infrastructure story at a time when that's the only macro wind at your back. The danger is that with Fear & Greed at 14.6, even strong names get hit on broad tape down days. These five are holding relative strength in a falling market, which is the exact filter you want — but market environment demands tighter stops and reduced size versus a neutral or bullish tape.

VG deserves a spot on the watchlist given energy sector momentum and IPO lifecycle structure — look for a defined intraday range in the first hour and enter on a break of that range to the upside. KRMN is observe-only Monday; the signal is weakest of the five and the macro backdrop doesn't reward reaching for marginal setups. Keep position sizes conservative across the board given the Extreme Fear reading — the names are right but the environment demands discipline on size and stops.
