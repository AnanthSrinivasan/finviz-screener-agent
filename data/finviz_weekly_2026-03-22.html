## Weekly Momentum Brief — March 16–22, 2026

**Top Names Breakdown**

SNDK is the standout of the week and earns its #1 slot on pure fundamental firepower. The January earnings report was a generational beat — $6.20 vs. $3.31 consensus, with forward guidance projecting EPS of $12–$14 next quarter against a prior-year loss. That's not just a beat, that's a complete re-rating event. The AI memory supercycle is the real engine here: hyperscaler capex is creating a structural NAND shortage that analysts don't see resolving before 2028, and SNDK as a pure-play spinoff from WDC is now the cleanest way to own that theme. The Jefferies $700 and KGI $992 price targets give institutional cover for continued buying. Seven days of screener persistence, 52-week high confirmation, and IPO lifecycle momentum all align. This is the highest-conviction name on the board.

VG gets to #2 on the strength of a genuinely stacked catalyst sequence compressed into a two-week window: an earnings beat, a Shell arbitration win, a new Trafigura LNG contract, and a geopolitical spike from the Qatar facility attack. Any one of those moves a midstream name — all four at once explains 7/7 persistence and a 10% Change screener hit. The sector read is also correct: USO +1.28% on the week and XLE +2.79% confirm energy is the one sector the market is actually rewarding right now. That said, RSI at 77.65 and price already above the $11.89 consensus target are real flags. This is extended. The thesis is intact but the entry window has likely passed for now. KRMN rounds out the IPO lifecycle trio with a cleaner fundamental story than its EPS line (-28%) suggests. The real catalyst is the guidance raise — FY26 revenue guided to $700–715M against a Street estimate of $578M at the time, a 20%+ beat that forces analyst model revisions and triggers institutional awareness buys. The Needham initiation and Piper Sandler upgrade arriving within a week of each other is textbook post-IPO discovery, exactly what IPO lifecycle screening is designed to catch early. Funded backlog up 30.8% and the Seemann Composites acquisition give the growth story real structural legs. Q67 Uptrend stage is the right read here.

LITE earns its spot via a different mechanism — this is an Episodic Pivot, not an IPO lifecycle play. The gap, 52-week high, and multi-screen combo on 5/7 days signals a discrete event-driven repricing rather than a slow institutional accumulation. At Q75 with 146.6% EPS growth, the fundamental quality is the best on this entire list. The 9.9% ATR is the highest here, which means the position sizing has to reflect that volatility — but for traders comfortable with wide stops, this is a high-quality setup in Communication Equipment at a time when XLK is only down modestly relative to the broader tape. ABVX closes the list as the weakest of the five — single screener (IPO only), lowest ATR at 4.9%, and 18.5% EPS growth that's real but not explosive. It belongs on a watchlist but not a Monday buy list. Q68 Uptrend stage keeps it respectable, but there's no discrete catalyst in the research to explain why it accelerates from here.

**Sector Themes and Macro Backdrop**

The macro environment is openly hostile to broad long exposure. Fear & Greed at 14.6 is Extreme Fear, all four major indices are down on the week and the month, and IWM is the most damaged with a -8.46% monthly drawdown. Gold and silver are getting hit hard — GLD -10.3% and SLV -15.4% on the week — which is an unusual risk-off signature that suggests forced liquidation rather than orderly rotation. The only sectors with genuine bids are energy (XLE +2.79%, USO +49.56% monthly) and, more modestly, financials. Technology is holding up relatively well on a relative basis but is still negative. This is a market where momentum concentration matters more than ever — the names that are working are working for specific, discrete reasons, not because of broad risk appetite. The AI infrastructure theme (SNDK) and LNG supply disruption (VG) are the two macro tailwinds strong enough to cut through the broader selling pressure.

**Monday Plan**

Three names to watch at the open. SNDK is the primary focus — look for a controlled pullback to the prior week's consolidation range or a flat open with volume confirmation above Friday's close. The 52-week high screener hit means price discovery is ongoing; don't chase a gap up, but any constructive base formation intraday is a valid entry with ATR-based stops. KRMN is the secondary setup — Q67 Uptrend, clean IPO lifecycle structure, and a guidance-driven re-rating that still has room to run as more analysts update models. Entry on a tight intraday flag or first pullback to VWAP. LITE is the speculative add — highest ATR on the list so position size accordingly, but Q75 quality and 146% EPS growth make it the best fundamental setup of the five if the episodic move has legs. Watch for a continuation open above last week's high rather than a gap-and-fade. Leave VG alone on Monday — extended by every measure and already above analyst targets. ABVX stays on the watchlist only.
