## Weekly Momentum Brief — Week of March 16–22, 2026

**Top Names Breakdown**

SNDK leads the board with a 195 score and deserves the top slot. This is a pure IPO lifecycle story with institutional acceleration: the WD spinoff cleared its final overhang on February 18 when Western Digital sold its last 7.5M shares, and the subsequent S&P 500 addition forced index fund buying into a stock with genuine fundamental velocity — $6.20 EPS vs. $3.31 consensus, 61% revenue growth, and forward guidance calling for $4.4–4.8B in Q3. Jefferies, Citi, and KGI all stacked price target raises in the same window, with KGI's $992 target the most aggressive. The NVIDIA GTC 2026 rack-scale NAND demand angle is a secondary catalyst that isn't yet in estimates. Seven days of persistence plus 52-week high plus IPO lifecycle plus multi-screen — every box checked. The EPS number showing -328% is likely a year-ago comparison distortion from pre-spinoff accounting, not a deterioration. ATR 7.5% means this moves — position size accordingly.

VG ties SNDK at 195 and the catalyst stack is legitimately dense. The Q4 EPS beat on March 2 triggered a 19.6% single-session gap. That gap was immediately reinforced by Qatar's LNG production halt from military strikes, a genuine geopolitical supply shock that repriced U.S. LNG export assets overnight. Then on March 13, VG reached FID on CP2 Phase 2 with $8.6B in committed financing and got a DOE export approval the same day — a trifecta of fundamental confirmation in 24 hours. BofA, Citi, Wells Fargo, and Scotiabank all raised targets within the same two-week window. The stock is up 53% in March alone, which flags it as extended on any reasonable entry framework. MACD is still constructive but chasing a 53% monthly move into Extreme Fear macro is not a Monday morning trade — this is a name to watch for a constructive pullback, not a new entry.

KRMN scores 185 and is the cleanest story in the group from a fundamental clarity standpoint. The Seemann Composites and MSC acquisition closed February 3, immediately expanding into Maritime Defense Systems and described as accretive across every financial metric in 2026. FY2026 guidance of $700–715M revenue and $205–215M EBITDA was set alongside the deal. Baird raised to $135 on March 20, Needham initiated Buy on March 13, Piper Sandler upgraded to Overweight in February — institutional coverage is building in the exact post-IPO accumulation pattern that screeners are designed to surface. The CEO transition was handled cleanly by the market. One flag: Q4 FY2025 earnings have not yet reported. That's a binary event — the guidance confirmation everyone is pricing in still needs to land. Q67 Uptrend stage is the strongest chart designation in this week's list.

LITE scores 161 on 5/7 days of persistence with a legitimate episodic pivot signal — gap, 52-week high, and multi-screen appearance on the same day. EPS growth of 146.6% is the best earnings quality number in the top 5. ATR of 9.9% is the highest here, meaning this name moves the most per day and requires tighter position sizing relative to dollar risk. Q75 is the highest Q-rank in the group — institutional quality is not in question. The communication equipment sector is benefiting from data center connectivity buildout, and LITE's optical components fit that theme directly. The 5/7 persistence (vs. 7/7 for others) and the episodic nature of the setup mean this is a breakout-from-base trade rather than a trending persistence trade — the entry framework is different. Watch for continuation above the gap high, not a moving average reclaim.

ABVX rounds out the list at 155 with IPO lifecycle driving the signal. EPS growth of 18.5% is real but modest compared to peers this week. ATR of 4.9% is the lowest in the group, which cuts both ways — less daily noise but also less explosive upside on a breakout. Q68 Uptrend stage is solid. The biotech sector (XBI down 1.25% on the week, down 5% on the month) is not providing a tailwind, which means ABVX is working against sector headwinds on its own momentum. It stays in the watchlist but is the lowest conviction entry of the five.

The one sector working is energy: XLE up 2.79% on the week and 7.48% on the month, USO up 49.56% on the month — that's the VG and broader LNG trade in sector form. The gold and silver collapse (GLD -10.3%, SLV -15.4% on the week) is violent and suggests a broader deleveraging or margin call dynamic rather than a clean rotation. When precious metals sell off that hard in a risk-off environment, it usually means forced selling somewhere in the system. Semiconductors (SMH -0.67% on the week) are holding better than the broader tape, which supports SNDK's relative strength thesis. Technology (XLK -1.1%) is also outperforming the index, consistent with LITE's continued screener presence. The critical point: these top 5 names are showing positive momentum in a week where nearly everything else sold off. That's relative strength that matters — but it also means entries need to be surgical, not aggressive.
